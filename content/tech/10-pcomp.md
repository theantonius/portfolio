Physical computing is the practice of building systems that sense and respond to the physical world, and it has been central to my work for over a decade and a half. I design and build with Arduino, Raspberry Pi,ESP32 and a range of sensors, connecting the digital and physical through hardware I wire, solder, and code myself. I taught physical computing at NYU Shanghai and work with students at all levels, from first breadboard to finished interactive installation.
